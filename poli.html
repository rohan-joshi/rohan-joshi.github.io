Aaji's Poli

Each time I remove these poli from the tawa I find myself wondering: Are they too dry? Too doughy? Not enough salt? Too much salt? 

It's these thoughts, more so than the poli itself, that makes me think of my Aaji, the materfamilias of this poli line.

Aaji is:

  • A spectacular poli maker
  • Spectacularly self-critical of everything she makes

    • So much so that in my transcription of her recipe — performed as she taught me to make rotis for the first time — I write:

  • "For maximum authenticity, it is suggested that you defer any compliments by responding that “it’s too dry”, or “I should have added more water,” or “I should have let it rise for longer,” etc. etc."

    • Of course, Aaji's poli are invariably perfect

    And so, years later, as I take yet another drab poli off the tawa, I find myself yearning for perfection lost (the poli yes, but really the person), comforted in some measure by the sense of her presence, lingering like the smell of the tawa deep into the friday night.
