England 2017

I had always seen England as this sort of idyllic place full of sweets, shrubbery, and sun-soaked countrysides. Augmented by my keeness for english literature, I was surprised to find many of my preconceived notions to be far from the truth.

My mother is British and by the grace of good fortune I had been to England a few times when I was younger. With arguably more observant and appreciative eyes, I came back to a country which shares both language and sport.

May 18, 2017

    Partly jetlagged but mostly disoriented with three hours to kill after I got off my flight, I made my way -luggage in hand- to Soho. To no one's surprise, it was raining.


    Soho's the former-red-light-district-turned-shopping-hub of London. Nearby, the Royal Academy of Arts .



May 19, 2017

    Still a bit jetlagged, I woke up a bit later than I had wanted. Walked to the Highgate Cemetery which wasn't all too far from where I was staying with my Uncle and Aunt. Highgate, located in North London, is fairly suburban and quite green despite being consider part of London, a seeming respite from the hustle and bustle of the central city. Highgate Cemetary is known for having housing the resting places of Karl Marx, Douglas Adams, and 170,000 other folks. 19th Century London parish burial grounds became overcrowded and thus seven private cemetaries were established to fight overcrowding. Highgate was one of them.


    In the afternoon, headed over to Alexandra Palace with my Aunt and Cousin. The Palace is currently an event venue but previously was a BBC Radio tower. During WWI, between 1915-1919 it served as an internment camp. History and purpose aside,there were some brilliant views of London from the top. So at least the German and Austrian citizens had that going for them at least.

May 20, 2017

    Haste was the theme of the day. With my Uncle and Cousin, we started at the Tate Modern having crossed over Blackfriars Bridge. After doing a short round of the Tate Modern with them, they left to go to a birthday party for a friend. I headed over the Millenium Bridge and walked to St. Paul's Cathedral. From there, took the tube to Carnaby Street in Soho for lunch. Afterwards, a walking tour of Marleybone. Marleybone was mainly hunting ground in the 17th, 18th, and 19th centuries but is now an affluent residential area. Shortly thereafter, walked to Baker Street, London Business School, Regent's Park, onto St. James' Park, Hyde Park, Buckingham Palace, House of Parliament, and eventually Big Ben. In the evening, I got a couple of pints with my Uncle at the local pub in Highgate.
